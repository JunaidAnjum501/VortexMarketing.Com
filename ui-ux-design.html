Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our UI/UX design services.

UI (User Interface) design focuses on the visual elements of a digital product, including buttons, icons, spacing, typography, color schemes, and responsive design. UX (User Experience) design is concerned with the overall feel of the experience and includes aspects like user research, content strategy, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing. While UI is about how the product looks, UX is about how it works. At Vortex Marketing, we integrate both disciplines to create digital products that are both visually appealing and highly functional.

The timeline for a UI/UX design project varies depending on the scope and complexity. A simple website redesign might take 4-6 weeks, while a complex application with extensive user research and testing could take 3-4 months. During our initial consultation, we'll assess your specific needs and provide a detailed timeline. Our process is transparent, and we keep you updated throughout each phase of the project to ensure we meet your deadlines.

We prioritize accessibility in all our designs by following WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) standards. This includes ensuring sufficient color contrast, providing text alternatives for non-text content, making all functionality available from a keyboard, and designing with screen readers in mind. We also conduct accessibility audits and testing throughout the design process to identify and address any potential barriers to access. Our goal is to create inclusive designs that can be used by everyone, regardless of their abilities.
